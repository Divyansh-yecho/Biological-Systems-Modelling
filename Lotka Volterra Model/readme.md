🐺🌿 Predator–Prey Dynamics — Lotka–Volterra Isocline Analysis

---

## 🧠 Overview

This project explores population dynamics using the Lotka–Volterra predator–prey model, with a specific focus on understanding and visualizing population isoclines. The goal is to study how predator and prey populations interact over time, and how equilibrium conditions emerge from these interactions.

By analyzing isoclines, the project provides insight into the conditions under which populations remain stable, grow, or decline, offering a geometric and intuitive understanding of the system dynamics.

---

## 🔬 The Model

The Lotka–Volterra equations describe the coupled evolution of prey and predator populations:

- Prey growth depends on natural reproduction and predation  
- Predator growth depends on food availability (prey) and natural decay  

These interactions lead to oscillatory dynamics and equilibrium points in the phase space.

---

## 📈 Isocline Analysis

The central focus of this project is the study of **isoclines**, which represent conditions where population change is zero:

- Prey isocline → where prey population remains constant  
- Predator isocline → where predator population remains constant  

The intersection of these isoclines defines the equilibrium point of the system.

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

1. Define the Lotka–Volterra differential equations  
2. Compute nullclines (isoclines) analytically  
3. Simulate population trajectories over time  
4. Plot phase space (prey vs predator)  
5. Visualize isoclines along with trajectories
